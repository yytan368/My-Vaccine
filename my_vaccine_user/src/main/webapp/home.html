Coronavirus (Covid-19) Vaccines Info
The Coronavirus (Covid-19) vaccines are safe and effective. They provide the best protection against COVID-19.
COVID-19 Booster Dose
A booster dose of Covid-19 vaccine is available for everyone aged 18 and above, and some people aged 16 and above, who have had 2 doses of the vaccine at least 3 months ago.


Types of Covid-19 Vaccines
The Covid-19 vaccines approved for use in the Malaysia are:
  • AstraZeneca Vaccine
  • Pfizer/BioNTech Vaccine
  • CoronaVac/ Sinovac Vaccine


How well do the COVID-19 vaccines work?
Anyone who gets COVID-19 can become seriously ill or have long-term effects. The COVID-19 vaccines are the best way to protect yourself and others as well.
Research has shown the vaccine helps:
  • Reducing the risk of getting seriously ill or dying from COVID-19
  • Reducing the risk of catching or spreading COVID-19.
  • Protect against COVID-19 variants
All the vaccines above mentioned require two shots whereby one shot provides only partial protection. Vaccines take times before protection reaches its maximum level of protection a few weeks after the second dose. Vaccinr do not provide 100% (full) protection.Therefore ,‘breakthrough infections’ – where people get the virus, despite having been fully vaccinated – will occur. Individuals should continue taking simple precautions even being vaccinated. More info about how to avoid catching and spreading COVID-19.


Side Effects and Safety
The COVID-19 vaccines approved for use in Malaysia have met strict standards of safety, quality and effectiveness. They can cause some side effects, but not everyone gets them. Any sied effects are usually mild and should not last longer than a week such as:
  • A sore arm from the injection
  • Feeling tired.
  • A headache
  • Feeling Icthy
  • Feeling or being sick
More serious side effects, such as allergic reactions or blood clotting, are very rare.


How to get your Covid-19 vaccine
If you are aged 18 or above you can:
  • Go to the registration page to register for the first dose vaccination.
  • Fill in all the required information and submit it.

If you cannot book appointments online, you can call the hotline given below. Do not hesitate to speak to our translator when necessary.

Booking your 2nd dose
People aged over 18 and aove are eligible for a 2nd dose. People at aged 18 and above should have their dose from 2 weeks later after their 1st dose. Most people of aged 16 or 17 should have their second dose from 4 weeks after their first dose.
  • If you book online, you will be asked to make appointments for the first dose. You can manage your Covid-19 vaccination appointments to view your appointments and rebook if necessary.
  • If you have done your first dose appointment, A confirmation will be sent to you when the appointment for second vaccination has assigned. The second dose appointment will be set within 3 days after your first vaccination.


Safety Measures of Covid-19
  • Weak a Mask.
  • Wash Your Hands Frequently.
  • Cough Etiquette. (Cover Your Mouth with Sleeve or Elbow)
  • Don't Touch Eyes, Nose, or Mouth with Unwashed Hand.
  • Avoid Contact with Sick People
  • Clean and Disinfect
  • Stay Home Stay Safe
